History & Mission

 

Mississippi Bangla Academy (MBA) was founded in 1997 by a group of Bengali people living in Mississippi. Recognizing the need to preserve and share their language and culture, the founders established the Academy with two primary objectives: to provide Bangla language and music lessons to local Bengali children, and to promote Bangla arts and culture within Mississippi and across the United States.

Over the years, Bengali community has grown rapidly, serving both Bengali families and individuals from diverse backgrounds who are interested in promoting Bengali heritage. The goals of this organization include offering lessons in Bangla language and fine arts, promoting cultural awareness, and fostering appreciation for Bangla arts and traditions. Beyond education, the MBA seeks to enhance social interaction, build friendships, and strengthen community relationships among Bengali and non-Bengali residents in Mississippi and throughout the U.S.

In recognition of its community service and cultural contributions, Mississippi Bangla Academy was officially registered as a non-profit organization in 2012. Today, it continues to serve as a cultural hub, dedicated to celebrating Bangla heritage, supporting education, and promoting meaningful connections across diverse communities.
