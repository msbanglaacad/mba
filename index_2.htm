Biography: I  was born in  Faridpur, Bangladesh and completed my high school education in my home town.  I graduated with  B.Sc. (Hons.) and M. Sc. in chemistry from the University of Dhaka, Bangladesh in 1988 and 1991, respectively.   Then I moved to Japan as a Monbusho scholar and joined the research group of Professor Kazuhiko Ichikawa at Hokkaido University. After graduation with my Ph.D in Supramolecular Chemistry (recognition of anions) in 1996, I was awarded an Alexander Humboldt postdoctoral research Fellowship and worked with Professor Hans-Jorg Schneider at Saarbrucken University, Germany where I studied peptide binding and ion pair recognition. In  1998,  I joined  as an Assistant Professor of Chemistry at the University of Dhaka, Bangladesh.  Since 2000, I have been working with the group of Professor Kristin Bowman-James  as a Postdoctoral Research Associate on the field of Supramolecular Chemistry of Anions.

 

 

Research Interest: Molecular Recognition and Supramolecular Chemistry

 

Model Receptor for Peptide: A strategy for binding of both C and N  terminal of unprotected  peptide has been developed by introducing of peralkylammonium ion and crown ether. Incorporation of a dansyl group provides further complementarity to side chain of peptide. Receptors for Biological and Environmental Relevant Anions: Anions play an important role in both biology and chemistry. It is known that more than 70% of enzymatic reactions in human body are involved with anions. Anions are also very important in medicine, and catalysis.  In environment excess anions e. g. nitrate and phosphate resulted from the use of fertilizer,   pollutant anions like sulfate, pertechnetate from nuclear waste, arsenate and fluoride from ground water are of concern.  Therefore the design and synthesis of suitable  receptors for anions is very important in both understanding the biochemical interaction and  in  separation and removal  process of such inorganic pollutant.  My research aims at the synthesis of  target receptors e.g.  Quaternary macrotricyclic per alkyl-ammonium ions, polyamine macro cycles and crypt ands.  Very recently we also focus on new type of  anion receptors like duel amine- amide and  amide-quaternary.

Binding Studies: High field NMR, UV and fluorescent spectroscopes are used for the analyses of binding contributions and conformations of supramolecular complexes in solution. Binding interaction is quantitatively determined by non-linear regression analysis of titration data.
